About Ethereum yield in this index

Ethereum is the original general-purpose smart-contract network and hosts the largest pool of liquidity and lending markets in DeFi. The strategies indexed here run on Ethereum mainnet rather than on rollups or sidechains.

We currently track 27 yield strategies on Ethereum, spanning USDC, ETH, USDT and BTC across 6 distinct protocols. The highest live APY in this set comes from USDC Alpha Core V2 at 17.02%; the average across the cohort is 4.28%.

Every strategy in the index is operated by Harvest Finance today; the Ethereumcohort therefore reflects Harvest's Ethereum deployments, not a survey of every yield source on the network. Inclusion criteria, APY and TVL handling, and ranking logic are documented on the methodology page.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best yield on Ethereum right now?

Across the 27 strategies we currently index on Ethereum, USDC Alpha Core V2 is showing the highest 24-hour APY at 17.02%. Rates change as on-chain conditions shift; the table above is sorted by 24-hour APY by default and reflects the most recent build.

How is yield generated on Ethereum?

Yield on Ethereum comes from the same set of mechanisms as elsewhere in DeFi: lending markets, liquidity provision on AMMs, leveraged staking, and incentive emissions. Each strategy in our index documents its specific source on its product page.

What protocols offer yield on Ethereum in this index?

The 27 Ethereum strategies we follow are deployed across 6 distinct protocols, including Morpho, Stake DAO, Fluid and Aave. The product table above shows the operator for each strategy.

What assets can I earn yield on via Ethereum?

Strategies in our Ethereum cohort accept deposits in USDC, ETH, USDT and BTC. Each product page lists the underlying asset and any reward tokens emitted by the strategy.

Is yield farming on Ethereum safe?

No DeFi yield strategy is risk-free. Smart-contract risk, oracle risk, liquidity risk, depeg risk, and governance risk all apply, and the same protocol can have different risk profiles on different networks. Per-strategy risk levels shown on this site are editorial classifications today; see the risk framework section of the methodology page for details.

How does Ethereum compare to other networks for DeFi yield?

Ethereum is one of several networks we cover. Comparisons across networks are most meaningful when controlled for asset (for example USDC on Ethereum versus USDC on Ethereum) because asset-specific markets dominate the rate. The "Yield on other networks" links below this page are the easiest way to make those comparisons.

How is APY on Ethereum calculated?

24-hour APY is the mean of APY records observed in the last 24 hours from our hosted indexer. 30-day APY is the simple arithmetic mean of daily APY observations over the last 30 days, filtering out negative values. Reward tokens contribute at the rates published by the underlying protocol; we do not perform our own USD conversion of reward streams. Full details are on the methodology page.
